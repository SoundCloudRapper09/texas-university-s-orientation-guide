Your College GPS exists because navigating college should not require piecing together information from a dozen different websites. Here is the story behind it.

The founders' story

Sambhav Khurana

Founder, Your College GPS

Finance student — UT Dallas, Naveen Jindal School of Management

Before I ever stepped foot on campus, I spent weeks trying to figure out what I was actually supposed to do before orientation. I checked my school's website, read blog posts, scrolled Reddit threads, and asked older students for advice. Every source gave me something different, and none of it was organized in a way that made sense for someone starting from zero.

I had already come in with some real-world experience. I had completed internships across finance, legal, and entrepreneurship before arriving on campus. I had co-founded a student organization, earned Eagle Scout, and captained varsity sports. But none of that prepared me for how scattered and confusing the pre-orientation process actually was.

"The information existed. It was just buried across a dozen websites, outdated forums, and word-of-mouth advice that changed depending on who you asked."

So I did what made sense to me as a finance student: I treated it like a research project. I went through each university's official admitted-student and orientation pages, pulled out exactly what mattered, verified the dates and requirements, and organized everything into a timeline built around priority and urgency. What started as a personal checklist for UTD became a resource for every major Texas university.

Your College GPS is the guide I wished existed when I was admitted. The goal is simple: no student should ever have to piece together their college journey from scratch. Whether you are preparing for the SAT, writing your application essays, figuring out orientation, or trying to land your first internship, this is where you come to get clear, sourced, school-specific guidance.

The mission

Five principles that drive every decision we make building Your College GPS.

School-specific, not generic
Every checklist, timeline, and guide is built for your specific university. General advice that could apply to any school is not useful when you are navigating one school's actual process.
Sourced from official pages only
Every item on every checklist links back to an official university source. No third-party blogs, no hearsay. If a university changes a requirement, we update it.
Timeline-driven
Knowing what to do matters. Knowing when to do it matters more. Every guide on this site tells you both, organized by priority so the most important things never get buried.
Built for every student
Whether you are the first person in your family to go to college or a student athlete managing a packed schedule, Your College GPS is designed to meet you where you are.
Always growing
Orientation checklists are just the beginning. The roadmap covers every stage from SAT prep to career placement, expanding to all 50 states as we go.
